# Gaming & Ergonomics

## Context
- Semi-professional ADC (League of Legends) — high APM role
- Plays most of free time — this is non-negotiable
- Right shoulder, forearm, and hand under constant repetitive strain
- Wakes up with fatigued/sore arms regularly
- This strain directly competes with gym recovery (especially push/pull)

## Strain Management Protocol
- Forearm stretches: 5 min before AND after each gaming session
  - Wrist flexor stretch (prayer position)
  - Wrist extensor stretch (reverse prayer)
  - Finger spreads
  - Wrist circles
- Gaming breaks: 5 min every 45-60 min (shake out arms, shoulder circles, stand up)
- Compression sleeves during extended sessions (optional)
- Topical magnesium on forearms before bed

## Recovery Interactions with Training
- Push/pull days tax the same muscles gaming uses → recovery conflict
- Light pump work (high rep, low weight) for arms aids recovery via blood flow
- Legs training does NOT interfere with gaming → can always be trained
- If arms are too fatigued to grip a bar → train legs or skip, don't force it

## Caffeine Connection
- Caffeine is a vasoconstrictor → reduces blood flow to forearms during gaming
- This worsens the repetitive strain and slows recovery
- Tadalafil 5mg offsets this vasoconstriction
- L-Citrulline also helps via nitric oxide pathway
- Caffeine cutoff by 4-5pm to protect sleep quality

## Pharmacological Options for Strain
- BPC-157 (250-500mcg/day): accelerates tendon/soft tissue repair. Sweet spot for chronic repetitive strain. Noticeable relief in 1-2 weeks anecdotally.
- TB-500: more systemic, slower, better for acute injuries. Overkill here.
- Alternatives: adequate protein, sleep, stretching routine, and scheduled breaks will do more than any peptide if consistently applied.
