# Recovery & Sleep

## Sleep Pattern
- Natural night owl: sleeps ~2:30-3:00am, wakes ~10:00am (~7-7.5hrs)
- This schedule is acceptable IF consistent and quality is good
- Total duration + quality > specific clock time
- Consistency (same window nightly) matters more than which window

## The Commute Problem (Critical Bottleneck)
- Day 1: wakes 5:30am after sleeping ~2:30am = **3 hours of sleep**
- This single night destroys recovery capacity for 2-3 days after
- Day 2: sleeps at friend's, normal schedule, but travels 3.5hrs home → arrives 8pm
- Mitigation: sleep earlier night before commute day (even midnight helps significantly)

## Recovery Limiters (ranked)
1. The 3-hour sleep commute night (biggest single factor)
2. Gaming-induced forearm/shoulder fatigue competing with training
3. Caffeine quality impact on deep sleep (even if falling asleep is fine)
4. Training volume vs. caloric intake mismatch (when cutting too hard)

## Optimization Levers
- Magnesium glycinate 400mg before bed → improves deep sleep %
- Caffeine cutoff by 4-5pm → protects sleep architecture
- Consistent sleep/wake times on WFH days → circadian stability
- Adequate calories → recovery requires energy surplus
- Hydration → 3L+ daily
- Ashwagandha → lowers cortisol, improves subjective recovery

## Signs of Under-Recovery
- Arms feel worse on waking (not just stiff — painful)
- Strength regression in the gym
- Motivation to train drops
- Diet adherence collapses
- Getting sick (immune suppression)

## What NOT to Do
- Don't try to become a morning person — it won't stick and will worsen adherence
- Don't add more training volume when recovery is the bottleneck
- Don't cut calories further when recovery is already compromised
